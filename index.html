Harriet Tubman

Harriet Tubman, born: March, 1822 died: March 10, 1913

Important Facts of Harriet Tubman's Life:

Harriet Tubman was born in approximately March 1822. Her given name was Arminta Harriet Ross. She was one of 9 children born to enslaved parents, Harriet "Rit" Green, and Ben Ross. Her mother was owned by Mary Pattison Brodess, and her father was owned by Anthony Thompson. Thompson and Brodess eventually married.

Harriet endured violence from a young age. She recalled getting 5 lashes one time before breakfast, which gave her lasting scars. Another incident occurred when she was sent on an errand to the dry goods store. Another slave had left the fields without permission, and the owner demanded Harriet help restrain the runaway. When Harriet refused, the man threw a brick and hit Harriet in the head. She suffered from epilesy, uncontrollable sleep episodes, and severe headaches after this event.

Harriet is most remembered for helping slaves to find freedom. She used the "Underground Railroad" to escape to freedom, and then went on to help her family and others to escape via the Underground Railroad themselves. You can read more about the Underground Railroad here.

In her later years, Harriet built a house for old people on her property. She eventually lived in the "Old Folks Home" until her death.

Proposed Currency Honoring Harriet Tubman:

In April, 2016 the Treasury Department announced that Harriet Tubman would replace Andrew Jackson on the twenty dollar bill. The release date is set for 2026, however, recently the Biden administration announced plans to speed up the process to release the new bills sooner than originally thought. Harriet Tubman deserves to be honored for her role in helping free slaves.
